10 filipino cuisine

Taste the vibrant and unique blend of sweet, sour and salty in every spoonful of Filipino cuisine.

about

Filipino cuisine is a melting pot of various cultural influences, with a strong Spanish, Chinese, and Malay heritage. The flavors are bold and savory, often featuring a combination of sweet, sour, and salty tastes. Some popular dishes include adobo, which is a type of meat or seafood cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, and spices, and sinigang, a sour soup made with tamarind and various meats or seafood. A popular dish is pancit, a type of noodle dish that can be made with various meats, seafood, or vegetables.

Desserts in Filipino cuisine are often made with tropical fruits such as mango, jackfruit, and coconut, and can be sweetened with ingredients such as condensed milk or sugar. Menudo is a hearty stew made with pork, potatoes, and chickpeas, it is a popular dish in the Philippines and is often served at special occasions and gatherings.

Bihon is a type of thin rice noodle commonly used in Filipino cuisine, it is a key ingredient in the dish pancit bihon, which is made with a variety of meats, seafood, and vegetables. Lugaw, also known as congee, is a type of rice porridge that is a comfort food in the Philippines, it is usually served with various meats, seafood, and toppings, and is also known as a remedy for colds and flu. Caldereta is a classic Filipino dish made with beef or goat meat, potatoes, and vegetables, cooked in a rich tomato-based sauce. All these dishes are the perfect example of the rich and diverse flavors that Filipino cuisine has to offer.

Overall, Filipino cuisine is a delicious blend of various cultural influences that is sure to delight any palate.

gallery

information

tinola

Chicken Tinola is a Filipino soup dish. It involves cooking chicken pieces in ginger broth. It is a popular cold weather dish. I always feel warm and cozy every time I eat it. This Filipino Chicken Soup is best enjoyed with fish sauce as dipping sauce, and a cup of warm white rice.

Kare Kare

Kare Kare is a type of Filipino stew with a rich and thick peanut sauce. It is a popular dish in the Philippines served during special occasions. The traditional recipe is composed of ox tail. There are instances wherein both ox tripe and tail are used. The vegetable components of the dish are string beans, eggplant, bok choy, and banana blossoms. Lightly browned toasted ground rice is used to thicken the sauce.

bulalo

It’s a well known fact that Filipinos love stew and soup dishes. From the sour sinigang to the sweet tinola, there’s guaranteed to be a soup dish for every flavor palate preference –– all great to sip and enjoy, especially during long, cool nights. But one of the most popular, and arguably most delicious, soups out there, is the classic Bulalo, or beef shank stew.

adobo

Chicken Adobo is a type of Filipino chicken stew. Chicken pieces are marinated in soy sauce and spices, pan-fried, and stewed until tender. The dish gained popularity because of its delicious taste and ease in preparation.

caldereta

Beef Kaldereta is a main stay in any Filipino Kitchen. It is a type of beef stew cooked with tomato sauce and liver spread. Ingredients such as potato, carrots, bell peppers, and olives are also utilized when cooking this dish.

arroz caldo

Chicken arroz caldo is a type of Filipino rice porridge with chicken slices. It is best served when topped with hard boiled eggs, some chopped scallions, and toasted or roasted garlic. I personally like to squeeze a piece of calamansi or lime over the toppings. It makes the dish taste fresher and delicious.

Pancit

Chicken Pancit Medley is an easy chicken pancit recipe that is made using two types of noodles, bihon and sotanghon. The first noodle is known as rice sticks. Pancit bihon is a good dish to make with it. Sotanghon is called green bean thread. This delicious pancit dish is quick and easy to make. It is also budget-friendly.

lumpia

Lumpiang Togue is a variation of the spring roll. Instead of meat, Mung bean sprouts (locally known as togue) are used as the major ingredient. This dish is a popular appetizer and snack in the Philippines and it is best eaten when dipped in spicy vinegar with onions and whole peppercorn.

sinigang

Sinigang is another classic Filipino dish that is well-loved by, of course, the Filipinos. This sour soup can brighten a gloomy day and Pinoys would even have this even on a hot summer night! Seriously, that’s how much they love this dish.

sisig

Crispy Tofu Sisig is a dish composed of deep fried tofu cubes, chopped chili peppers, and onion. It is healthier alternative to traditional pork sisig because it does not use any meat and greasy ingredients. Aside from being healthy, this crispy tofu sisig recipe is also vegan.
